About
Experiences | Education | Awards

Shaun Wang is a second-year student at Columbia University’s specialized program (MODA), which focuses on critical and curatorial methods of cultural and art history. He is also developing data science skillsets at Columbia University’s Data Analytics and Visualization Bootcamp, and hopes to intersect his humanities foundation and Data Science skill set. His intention is to continue his education by obtaining a second accelerated master's in Data Science during the year of 2021 and begin full-time employment in 2022. 

His experience ranges from working with Oxo Group as an Analytics and Research Consultant, assisting demographic research and company oversea fillings, to, working with MVRDV and BCA as a Strategy consultant to develop a SWOT analysis on a $300 million cultural real estate development. Furthermore, he has also worked an Investment Analyst at Recharge Capital (VC) and as a Strategy Analyst at Startup thread.io. Parallel to his plan of obtaining dual master's degrees, His biggest interest lies in the intersection of humanities and data science, where he is currently working as a research assistant at Columbia University’s Media center, assisting on the processes of computer vision. 

His most prideful accomplishment lies in his research on Chinese private museums, which crystalized into a book and was published by Columbia Global Center. The project received ¥190,000 in combined sponsorships from Asia Art Archive, New Century Art Foundation, SAIC, Chao Art Center, and Shanghai Glass Museum. It is featured in 5 major news organizations and sold over 400 copies in the first week.
