ML Model: "Amazon Vine Bias Testing"

In this project, I wanted to see if we can use ML models as testing method to see if Amazon's Vine Program is biased in their reviews. I used PySpark, AWS RDS/S3, and Postgres to retrieve over 1.5 million amazon furniture reviews. I performed ETL processes on the dataset, stored the results on AWS RDS database, and used NLP's TD-IDF to examine if a ML model can predict if a review belongs within Amazon's Vine Program based on product reviews /'Review Body'.

Problem

While we can use descriptive statistics to measure the differences in Vine Program reviews based on differences in star_ratings, our human capabilities can only compare variables that are numericalitically categorized. Due to the size of the dataset, it is humanly possible to deduce that there is biases within large sets of textual reviews. A difference in average star_ratings between Vine and non-Vine reviews could be due to various factors (such as, reviewer's product selection,

For these reasons, we must rely on ML models.

Our most important metric in determining Vine program's baises is based upon our Multiclass Classificiation Evaluator (MCE). The MCE evaluates how well the model performas at determinig if the review belongs to the Vine program. This means that if the model has lesser accurary the more unbias these reviews are. Inversely, a high MCE implies a larger bias.

Scope

What is Amazon's Vine Program?

"Amazon Vine invites the 'most trusted reviewers' on Amazon to post opinions about new and pre-release items to help their fellow customers make informed purchase decisions. Amazon invites customers to become Vine Voices based on their reviewer rank, which is a reflection of the quality and helpfulness of their reviews as judged by other Amazon customers. Amazon provides Vine members with free products that have been submitted to the program by participating vendors. Vine reviews are the independent opinions of the Vine Voices. The vendor cannot influence, modify or edit the reviews. Amazon does not modify or edit Vine reviews, as long as they comply with our posting guidelines. A Vine review is identified with the green stripe Customer review from the Amazon Vine Program." (Copied from Amazon Vine Website)

Our processes will also follow the folling processes:

01.

Extract

The first step is to extract the 1.5 million rows of Amazon Furniture Ratings through its open-source API.

02.

Extract

Transform the data as seperate dataframes within PySpark Dataframes In our case, we need the 'Review Bodies' as our feature

03.

Classification

Using a pipeline, we tokenize, remove stop words, hash, and td-idf our 'review bodies dataframe. This is to preprare for it to become ML features.

04.

Train

Using the features we have created, we split the data into 70% training and 30% testing.

05.

Evaluate

We will evaluate our model based upon Naive Bayes's method inorder to test its accuracy.
